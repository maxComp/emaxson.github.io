Finnish Immigrants

Arrived in the 1890's

The first Finnish immigrants arrived in Long Valley in the 1890s. They came from eastern Oregon where they had tried homesteading. Long Valley with its forests, mountains and green meadows appealed to them more than the Oregon desert country.

Most of the Finns came to Long Valley between 1900 and 1925. The largest Finnish community, Elo, was on the Elo Road southeast of present-day McCall.

Named for its religious leader and teacher, Rev. John Eloheimo, Elo had a store, post office, school, and a meeting hall. Many of Elo's Finns were Lutherans.
