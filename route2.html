Motor car ride described

Idaho Statesman August 5, 1915

Those going from Boise by auto may be interested in the following information given by a member of a party escorted to the lake recently by Dr. Titus, and that included Dr. and Mrs. King and Mr. Moholin. It is related that the best route is via Star and Emmett, although this party went from Star direct to Montour via the "Scotch Bob" ranch.

The road from the latter place to Montour is rough and slow. Going by way of Emmett to Montour, while longer would be more comfortable and probably save time. From Montour the route lays via Sweet.

Above Sweet the party took the upper Dry Buck road, following the Dry Buck Lumber company's signs. The rest of the road is by way of Smith's Ferry, Thunder City and Roseberry.

The distance from Boise by the route here indicated is 125 miles. The time required is from seven to 12 hours, according to machine conditions. With a short coupled car, especially a roadster, it is far more satisfactory to make a day of it. With the large touring car it can be made easily in eight hours. The record is given at slx and a half hours.
