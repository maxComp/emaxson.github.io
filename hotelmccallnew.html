OPEN TO PUBLIC

Idaho Statesman September 1, 1938

McCALL (Special) - The new modern Hotel McCall held its official opening August 31. William Deinhard, owner of the former McCall hotel which burned early this year, is owner and manager. Wally McGill its chief clerk.

The hotel has 29 rooms, part with bath. All are furnished with modernistic furniture. Each room is equipped with radio connections and electric razor plug-ins. The bathrooms have the latest fixtures with built-in bath tubs and combination tubs and showers.

The outside is of stucco finish and is fireproof. A large sign extends the length of the front of the building. The lobby, which has not yet been completed, will be furnished with chrome streamlined furniture. A sitting room adjoins the lobby overlooking Payette lake. The view of the lake is especially lovely from the corner rooms.

A ski room is being equipped in the basement where skier's may wax skis, dry clothes, store skis and skates, etc. A suite of four rooms will be furnished for Mr. and Mrs. Deinhard. The entire floor is carpeted. The hotel is open to the general public September 1.
